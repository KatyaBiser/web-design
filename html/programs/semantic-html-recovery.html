Semantic HTML Recovery

4 weeks • Beginner to Intermediate • Led by Dr. Emma HTML

Program Overview

Let's talk about your div problem. You know the one. That nested nightmare where `<div class="container"><div class="wrapper"><div class="inner"><div class="content">` is just how you start every project. You've been using divs for EVERYTHING. Headers? Divs. Navigation? Divs with onclick handlers. Buttons? Believe it or not, also divs. You're basically building websites with a single HTML element and calling it "minimalist."

"But divs work fine!" you protest, as screen readers announce "Group. Group. Group. Group." for 47 consecutive divs. Meanwhile, search engines look at your site and think it's a blank page because there's zero semantic structure. Your HTML looks like someone discovered the `<div>` tag and decided that was all they needed to learn. Spoiler: HTML has 142 elements. You've been using 3 of them.

This intensive 4-week program, led by Dr. Emma HTML (who has seen div-soup nightmares that would make you weep), will teach you that HTML actually has MEANING. `<header>` exists. `<nav>` exists. `<article>` exists. They're not just "fancy divs"—they actually DO something for accessibility, SEO, and maintainability. Shocking, we know.

What You'll Learn

  • That HTML has 142 elements, not just div, span, and p
  • <header>, <main>, <footer> - the holy trinity you've been ignoring
  • <nav> for navigation (not `<div class="nav">`, Karen)
  • <article> vs <section> (yes, they're different, no, they're not interchangeable)
  • <button> for buttons (divs with onclick are not buttons!)
  • Heading hierarchy: h1-h6, not just "make it big" with CSS
  • <time> for dates (so machines can actually read them)
  • <figure> and <figcaption> (images can have captions, who knew?)
  • Forms that screen readers don't hate: <label>, <fieldset>, <legend>
  • Why your div-soup is killing your SEO and accessibility
  • The "Div Detox" process: refactoring 200 divs into 30 semantic elements

Prerequisites

This program is for developers who:

  • Think `<div class="header">` is the same as `<header>` (it's not)
  • Have never met an HTML element they couldn't replace with a div
  • Believe "semantic HTML" is just a buzzword (oh honey, no)
  • Use divs as buttons and wonder why keyboard users are mad
  • Have HTML that makes screen readers announce "Group. Group. Group."
  • Think h1-h6 are just "font sizes, but semantic"
  • Want their SEO to not be garbage
  • Are ready to face the truth about their div addiction
  • Can commit 8-10 hours per week to recovering from div-soup trauma

Program Format

Each week includes:

  • 2 live therapy sessions (2 hours each) - Tuesday and Thursday evenings (attendance mandatory for div detox)
  • Div counting exercises - Audit your worst projects and face the truth (approximately 3 hours per week)
  • Refactoring workshops - Learn to replace 200 divs with 30 semantic elements (1 hour group session)
  • Screen reader testing - Hear what you've inflicted on users (prepare tissues)
  • Code review sessions - Dr. Emma HTML reviews your markup (1 hour individual feedback)
  • Support group access - Connect with other div-soup survivors 24/7
  • "I'm Sorry" ceremony - Week 4 graduation where you apologize to all the divs you've misused
