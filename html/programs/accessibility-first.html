Accessibility First

5 weeks • All Levels • Led by Dr. Aria Accessible

Program Overview

"It works on MY machine!" you say proudly, as your website is completely unusable for 15% of the internet. Your beautiful gradient button? Invisible to screen readers. Your fancy custom dropdown? Keyboard users are crying. Your color scheme? People with color blindness see nothing. But hey, it looks great on your 27-inch 4K monitor, right?

You've been shipping websites that work perfectly... for you. Meanwhile, screen reader users hear "button button button image link button" and give up. Keyboard navigators get trapped in your modal and can't escape. People with motor disabilities can't click your 8-pixel touch targets. But sure, that parallax effect is totally worth excluding millions of users.

This 5-week program, led by Dr. Aria Accessible, will teach you that accessibility isn't a "nice to have"—it's literally the law in many places, and more importantly, it's basic human decency. Dr. Accessible once made a developer use their own website with a screen reader. The developer cried. Then they fixed their entire codebase.

What You'll Learn

  • Understanding WCAG 2.1 AA standards (yes, they exist for a reason)
  • Using semantic HTML so screen readers don't hate you
  • ARIA attributes: when to use them and when to stop making things worse
  • Keyboard navigation: because not everyone uses a mouse, shocking I know
  • Color contrast ratios: light gray on white isn't "minimalist," it's illegible
  • Focus management: where did the keyboard focus go? Nobody knows!
  • Alt text that's actually useful (not "image" or "pic1.jpg")
  • Testing with actual screen readers (prepare for humility)
  • Building forms that don't make people rage-quit

Prerequisites

This program is for developers who:

  • Think accessibility is "for later" (spoiler: later never comes)
  • Have never used a screen reader on their own website
  • Believe "it looks fine" is an accessibility standard
  • Use div and span for everything (semantic HTML who?)
  • Think ARIA is a Game of Thrones character
  • Have light gray text on white backgrounds for "aesthetics"
  • Can't tab through their own website without getting lost
  • Are ready to commit 8-10 hours per week to not excluding people

Program Format

Each week includes:

  • 2 live sessions (2 hours each) - Tuesday and Thursday evenings
  • Screen reader practice - Use NVDA, JAWS, or VoiceOver on your projects
  • Accessibility audits - Review and fix real websites
  • Code review sessions - 1 hour group feedback
  • Guest speakers - People who actually use assistive technologies
  • Community support - Help each other fix accessibility sins

Expected Outcomes

By the end of this program, you will be able to:

  • Build websites that work for everyone, not just you
  • Use a screen reader confidently to test your work
  • Meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards without crying
  • Write semantic HTML that makes sense
  • Use ARIA correctly (and know when not to use it)
