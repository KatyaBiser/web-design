Dr. Emma HTML

Semantic Markup Specialist

Biography

Dr. Emma HTML has been fighting the good fight against div-soup for over 20 years. With a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Stanford University and extensive experience in web standards, she has helped thousands of developers rediscover the power and elegance of semantic HTML.

Her approach combines technical rigor with compassion, understanding that many developers fell into bad habits not through laziness, but through the overwhelming pressure to deliver quickly. She believes that with proper guidance and support, any developer can learn to write meaningful, accessible markup.

Before joining Rehab from Vibe, Dr. HTML worked at the W3C contributing to HTML5 specifications and consulted for Fortune 500 companies on web accessibility compliance.

Areas of Expertise

  • Semantic HTML5 elements and document structure
  • Web accessibility (WCAG 2.0/2.1 compliance)
  • Progressive enhancement strategies
  • SEO optimization through semantic markup
  • Form design and validation
  • ARIA roles and attributes
  • Microdata and structured content

Treatment Approach

Dr. HTML uses a structured, evidence-based methodology that helps developers understand not just how to write semantic markup, but why it matters. Her sessions typically include:

  • Code review and analysis of current practices
  • Interactive exercises building real-world components
  • Accessibility testing with assistive technologies
  • Pair programming sessions focusing on semantic refactoring
  • Weekly homework assignments with detailed feedback
