Dr. Emma HTML

Semantic Markup Specialist & Div-Soup Survivor Advocate

Biography

Dr. Emma HTML's journey began in the dark days of 2005, when she witnessed a perfectly good website being built entirely with nested `<div>` tags. "Divs all the way down," she recalls with a shudder. "No `<header>`, no `<nav>`, no `<article>`. Just... divs. Hundreds of them." That traumatic experience sparked her lifelong mission to heal div-soup victims.

Her revolutionary "Semantic Awakening Protocol" helps developers rediscover that HTML has MORE than just div and span. "I've seen grown engineers cry tears of joy when they discover `<section>` exists," she says. Her gentle approach recognizes that many fell into div-soup not from laziness, but from following AI suggestions that treated HTML like a generic container factory.

Before founding the Semantic Recovery Institute, Dr. HTML worked at W3C where she personally lobbied for adding more semantic elements to HTML5. Her famous quote: "If I see one more `<div class='header'>` when `<header>` exists, I'm going to scream into the void."

Treatment Approach

Dr. HTML's compassionate methodology recognizes that div addiction is real and valid. Her evidence-based treatment includes:

  • Safe space for admitting you've used <div class="button">
  • Guided semantic element discovery workshops
  • 12-step program: "From Div-Dependent to Semantically Sober"
  • Pair coding sessions: refactoring div-soup together
  • Screen reader empathy exercises (hearing your own code read aloud)
  • Weekly "Element of the Week" support groups
  • Emergency hotline for "I'm about to wrap everything in divs" moments
